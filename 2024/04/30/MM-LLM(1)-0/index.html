MM-LLM(1)

Attention: The content of this article is basically from the paper MM-LLMs: Recent Advances in Multi-Modal Large Language Models.

Objective

The Objective of MM-LLM is to harness LLMs as the cognitive powerhouse to empower various MM tasks.

The Core Issue

The core challenge facing MM-LLMs is effectively connecting LLMs with models in other modalities to enable collaborative inference.

Training Method

MM Pre-Training (PT) + MM Instruction-Tuning (IT) pipeline.

Model Structure

  • The Modality Encoder, LLM Backbone, and Modality Generator are generally frozen during training.
  • The primary optimization emphasis is on Input and Output Projectors.
  • Given that Projectors are lightweight components, the proportion of trainable parameters in MM-LLMs is notably tiny compared to the total parameter count (typically around 2%).

The Input Projector can be achieved directly by a Linear Projector or Multi-Layer Perceptron (MLP), i.e., several linear projectors interleaved with non-linear activation functions. There are also more complex implementations like Cross-attention, Q-Former (Li et al., 2023e), P-Former (Jian et al., 2023), and MQ-Former (Lu et al., 2023a). However, both Q-, P-, and MQ-Former require an additional PT process for initialization.
